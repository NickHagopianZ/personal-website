Frisbee

Nick started playing frisbee for Wooster Men's Ultimate during his sophomore year of college. The team ("Ramjam") practices 3-5 times a week year-round. They also compete in weekend tournaments, culminating in a sectional tournament which allows for advancement to regionals and then nationals.

During his senior year, Nick acted as treasurer for his college team. His role was to coordinate with administration on behalf of Ramjam. In this role he supported the largest non-Greek organization on campus. He also organized travel, housing and funding for over 50 students.

Nick continues to play when possible, often joining summer league or reuniting with college friends for tournaments.

    • Volunteer Work

    During his time at the College of Wooster Nick volunteered weekly as part of the Wooster Volunteer Network while still completing a full course load. The organizations he worked with were the Boys & Girls Club of America and the Nick Amster Workshop.

    The mission of Nick Amster, Inc. is to ensure that every adult with a developmental disability or special health care need thrives in the community of their choice. As the premier provider of adult services, our compassionate and experienced staff collaborate with families, caregivers, and local partners to provide opportunities for individuals to live, learn, work, and achieve their highest level of independence.

    During his time with the Nick Amster Workshop, Nick participated in coordinating and participating in events to provide individuals social and recreation opportunities.
